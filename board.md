## Leadership

Dr. Corrado Garbazza serves as President and Dr. Renske Lok as Vice President for 2024–26. Dr. Christian Cajochen joins the society's list of past presidents.

## Board members

<div class="card">
  <h3>Dr. Beatriz Bano-Otalora</h3>
  <p>A Lecturer in Physiology and member of the Centre for Biological Timing at the University of Manchester. She completed her PhD in Circadian Physiology at the University of Murcia, Spain, then moved to the UK as a postdoc to study the neurobiology of circadian clocks and how daytime lighting affects circadian rhythmicity in diurnal animal models. Since taking up a Lectureship at Manchester in 2022, she's been building an independent research group focused on how early-life light exposure shapes the development of the circadian clock in diurnal species.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <h3>Dr. Rebecca Cox</h3>
  <p>A clinical psychologist and director of the Sleep, Circadian Rhythms, and Psychopathology Laboratory at Washington University in St. Louis.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <h3>Dr. John Hanifin</h3>
  <p>Associate Director of the Thomas Jefferson University Light Research Program. In addition to serving as Project Manager on NIH- and NASA-sponsored research studies, he was recently awarded a Department of Energy grant examining how solid-state lighting affects human physiology. Using radioimmunoassay and performance-testing techniques, his research has explored how polychromatic blue-light mixtures affect circadian, neuroendocrine, and neurobehavioral outcomes in humans.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <h3>Dr. Ali Amidi</h3>
  <p>Associate Professor in Neuroscience and Psychology at Aarhus University's Department of Psychology &amp; Behavioural Sciences. He completed his PhD in Neuroscience and Health Psychology at Aarhus and is a leading member of the university's Unit for Psycho-Oncology and Health Psychology.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <h3>Dr. Manuel Spitschan &amp; Delainey Wescott</h3>
  <p>Joined the board alongside Dr. Bano-Otalora, Dr. Cox, and Dr. Amidi, bringing the board's expertise across the full translational spectrum from basic to clinical science.</p>
</div>
